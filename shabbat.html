What is Shabbat?

Shabbat or the Sabbath, also called Shabbos by Ashkenazim, is Judaism's day of rest on the seventh day of Saturday

6 of 39 things not permitted on Shabbat

  1. transporting an object (between private and public domains, or over 4 cubits within public domain
  2. extinguishing a fire
  3. kindling a fire
  4. baking
  5. tearing
  6. slaughtering

Sources for Shabbat in the Bible

  1. Sabbath is given special status as a holy day at the very beginning of the Torah in Genesis 2:1-3
  2. Relating to the distance one may travel by foot on the Sabbath in Exodus 20:8-11
  3. It is the first holy day mentioned in the Bible, and God was the first to observe it with the cessation of creation (Genesis 2:1-3>
  4. The categories of labors prohibited on Shabbat are exegetically derived – on account of Biblical passages juxtaposing Shabbat observance (Exodus 35:1-3
